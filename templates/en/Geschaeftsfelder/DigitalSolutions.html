Digital Solutions

The integration of digital services and software in buildings already plays a crucial role today and is shaping the way we design, construct, and use buildings. Similar to the revolution in the automotive sector—where sensors, actuators, and digital services have become standard—buildings are increasingly relying on these technologies to optimize functionality, efficiency, and user experience.

The importance of software engineering and apps is particularly evident in enhancing building usability. With customized apps, users can make personalized settings for room temperature, lighting, and other environmental conditions. These apps, integrated into the building management system, automatically adapt to users' individual preferences and habits, increasing comfort while also improving efficiency by aligning with occupants’ needs. Building security is enhanced through advanced software solutions such as biometric access controls, surveillance cameras, intelligent alarm systems, and motion pattern analysis. These digital services help increase building security and prevent unauthorized access.

In the networking of buildings, not only the building itself is considered, but also the surrounding infrastructure is taken into account. The development of software solutions and APPS plays a key role here in ensuring seamless interaction and communication between the various subsystems. By linking traditional operational data with information from different subsystems, new opportunities emerge for gaining comprehensive insights and designing more efficient building systems. The true added value lies in the development of new digital services. The extensive analysis of combined data enables the creation of innovative solutions—be it in the form of personalized user experiences, automatic energy optimization, or intelligent space planning concepts. Digital services rely on data availability both inside and outside the building. The data of a building is an asset. Through digital services, this data can generate additional revenue. Data and digital services can therefore significantly influence the value of a building.

The integration of artificial intelligence (AI) and machine learning plays a central role in building automation. These technologies identify energy consumption patterns, enable predictive maintenance, and continuously optimize building management. By creating digital twins of buildings, virtual models can be synchronized with physical buildings in real time. This facilitates monitoring, maintenance, and the simulation of changes before they are implemented physically.
